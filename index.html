What does a Caregiver do?

A caregiver is a professional who helps an individual with daily living activities. Caregiving normally occurs when someone reaches old age, has a disability or disease or has a mental disorder and might need extra help.

A caregiver traditionally works in a one-on-one environment with a patient or person who needs care. This could be in their home or the home of a relative or a designated place of residence. Additionally, a caregiver or companion may work in a nursing home or health facility.

Feedback

Working as a Caregiver

There are many tasks that a caregiver might be called upon to do.

A caregiver may:

  • Grocery shop, cook, clean the house or do laundry
  • Provide transportation
  • Help their patient get dressed, remind them to take medicine or help with bathing
  • Help with physical therapy, arranging and aiding with appointments, talking with medical staff or handling crises
  • Handle finances or other legal affairs or to just be a companion
